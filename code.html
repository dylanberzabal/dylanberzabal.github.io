The AIDS Crisis & Felix Gonzalez-Torres:
An Archive of Love, Loss, and Life

This website is a growing archive of various texts, images, and artifacts from the height of the AIDS Crisis in the 80s and 90s, organized thematically and explored through the lens of works by artist Felix Gonzalez-Torres. Felix Gonzalez-Torres was a Cuban American artist. Active in the 80s and 90s, much of his work revolves around the AIDS Crisis and death of his partner, Ross Laycock to the virus in 1991.

The LGBTQ+ community has had a difficult and complicated history, with the 80s and 90s being especially turbulent due to the AIDS Crisis. Unfortunately, queer history is seldom taught in schools, and there is a vast lack of proper historical acknowledgement of major queer figures in history. This dismissal is not only unfair to those of the past, but also to current queer youth who may feel disconnected with their identity and community.

This website aims to educate younger generations, especially queer youth, about a tumultuous period whoâ€™s effects are still being felt today. In doing so, my hope is that it not only assists with the continued formation of their identities and communities, but also inspires them to continue building upon the work done by those before them.

Goodnight, I love you.

Goodnight, I love you. is an exploratory narrative driven experience. The player is dropped into a navigable world with no given information about the narrator, thus tasked with piecing together the information they can gather from objects in the environment.

What seems like a typical case of restlessness turns out to be something else entirely, as the player navigates various themes surrounding adolescence, loneliness, aimlessness, empathy, belonging, and what it means to grow up.
